# About

I am a recent Master’s graduate in Computer Science focused on building practical AI systems at the intersection of software engineering, machine learning, and natural language processing. My recent work has centered on developing AI prototypes that do more than produce outputs — they retrieve evidence, reason over language, evaluate model behavior, and expose limitations in systems that people may rely on.

My path into computer science began with a background in languages, linguistics, and applied communication. That background continues to shape how I approach AI: I am especially interested in systems that interpret human language, support better decision-making, and remain transparent enough for people to understand and trust.

During my Master’s program, I worked on projects involving multi-agent LLM systems, generative AI, robustness evaluation, social media analysis, NLP pipelines, and evolutionary algorithms. These experiences helped me develop a practical engineering mindset: define the problem clearly, build a working system, evaluate it carefully, document tradeoffs, and improve from the results.

Although many of my projects are AI and machine-learning focused, my broader goal is to grow as a Software Engineer or AI Engineer. I enjoy building the infrastructure around intelligent systems — data pipelines, model workflows, evaluation tools, backend logic, and user-facing applications that make AI useful in real contexts.
