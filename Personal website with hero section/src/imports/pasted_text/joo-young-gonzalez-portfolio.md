Design a polished, responsive personal portfolio website for **Joo-Young Catherine Gonzalez**, a recent Master of Computer Science graduate pursuing **Software Engineer and AI Engineer roles**.

The website should position her as an engineer who builds practical AI systems that connect research ideas to working software. Her work focuses on natural language processing, generative AI, trustworthy machine learning, multi-agent systems, and social media analysis.

## Primary Goal

Create a professional portfolio that:

* Presents Joo-Young as both a software engineer and an AI engineer
* Makes complex technical projects understandable to recruiters and hiring managers
* Highlights engineering decisions, system design, evaluation, and measurable outcomes
* Communicates her background in language, linguistics, and human-centered AI
* Can grow over time as new projects, skills, and experience are added
* Encourages visitors to view her projects, résumé, GitHub, LinkedIn, or contact her

## Design Direction

Use a modern, technical, and approachable visual style.

The design should feel:

* Professional but not overly corporate
* Technical but still accessible to non-specialist recruiters
* Thoughtful, trustworthy, and research-oriented
* Clean, spacious, and easy to scan
* More like an engineer’s product portfolio than an academic résumé page

Avoid:

* Excessive AI imagery, robots, glowing brains, or generic neural-network graphics
* Overly futuristic neon styling
* Dense walls of text
* Too many animations
* Skill percentage bars
* A layout that resembles a résumé copied directly onto a webpage

Use a light or neutral background with strong typography, subtle borders, restrained accent colors, and generous spacing. A deep blue, teal, muted violet, or similar accent may be used to suggest technology and trust.

Use a modern sans-serif typeface. Code-style or monospace typography may be used sparingly for labels, technical metadata, or project technologies.

## Accessibility

Ensure:

* Strong color contrast
* Readable body text
* Visible keyboard focus states
* Clear heading hierarchy
* Buttons are distinguishable from normal links
* Information is not communicated through color alone
* Motion is subtle and optional
* The layout remains understandable without decorative imagery
